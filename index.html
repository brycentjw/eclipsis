What is Eclipsis?

Eclipsis is a planet, and the name of the game in Roblox, the objective of the game is to clear map of other teams.
You can decide to play yourself, or form a team, only play solo if you're extremely experienced in the game itself.

Core game

Here I will talk about the core of the game, you will typically see people talk about this.

Rating

Rating is a factor people will typically judge you by, you lose a match, you lose rating, you win a match, you win rating.
How much you lose or gain has several details, which I will explain here.
First of all, you will always start with 1200 rating.
Second of all, if you win a match your rating will increase depending on:

  • Your rating compared to others (you gain more from others if they have more rating than you)
  • Number of enemies in your match (you collectively gain rating from the above factor counting for each user)
  • Number of people on your team (rating is evenly distributed among your team)

The first point will be explained, if you have 1200 rating and you win against another player with 1200 rating,
you will get 3 ratings from that one player (if you're playing alone).
If you have 1200 rating and you beat somebody with less rating,
you will either gain 1 or 2 depending on how little they have.
If you have 1200 rating and you beat somebody with more rating,
you will gain 4, 5, or 6 rating depending on how much more they have than you.
If you have 3 enemies and you're playing yourself in a 1v3 situation and somehow win,
this system will apply to each enemy user individually, and will total up to the amount you will receive.
If you have teammates, however, the amount of rating will be divided among your teammates,
for example if your 3 man team receives 30 rating, each user (including you) will get 10 rating.
However, if your team gains 2 rating but you are a 5 man team, each user (including you) will get 1 rating.
If you lose a match, your rating will decrease depending on:

  • Your rating compared to others (you lose more to others if they have less rating than you)

Now it's just the opposite from winning a match, only this one factor will affect you. Nothing much to explain.

How to play


Now you have figured out the 'points' system, time for you to start figuring out how to play.
As you spawn in, you will be on what is called a 'Starter Island',
this will have a single unclaimed well pipe and a fully filled up spawn point with 350 iridium in it.
Now as you may choose to stay here, it's a bad idea,
as there is only a single well pipe which can only be upgraded enough to do 12.5 ir/s.
Well pumps can be placed for 100 iridium, and then upgraded from there to make (in order):

  1. Fabricator
  2. Distributor
  3. Extra pumping power (boosts extractor power from 5 ir/s to 12.5 ir/s)

Now that I have explained how a well pump works,
there are 2 choices which will do well easily for early game,
you can either make a sea base, or expand onto mainland.
I will talk about expanding onto mainland first, as it is much easier to master.

Mainland expansion


Expanding onto the mainland, you can find clusters of well pipes of potentially 4 or 5,
with a potential of 50ir/s to 62.5 ir/s respectively.
To get there, you will need to build platform bridges, which are incredibly cheap, but easy to destroy.
Once you reach the cluster, building there will allow for you to setup an easy amount of production,
where it is still possible for you to find even more clusters of well pipes, to boost production.
Going any further will progress into mid game, where conflict will often initiate between teams.
I will now explain how you make sea bases, which is much harder to master than simply placing well pumps.

Sea basing

Making sea bases is exceptionally dangerous if you do not know how to build it.
You can either jump into the ocean and land on a pillar, or climb down using a ladder.
After reaching the ocean, remember to stay above the toxic fumes which will damage you,
depending on how near you are to the said ocean.
Place a wall sideways, with the side inside the ocean, while the top is still above the fumes which is somewhat visible.
Submersibles will produce iridium while inside the ocean, and the entire rod must be submerged for a potential of 12 ir/s,
which is extremely powerful, especially with the fact you can easily pull iridium from the ocean to fill in the need for 650 iridium.
Place Submersibles on the side of the wall, with the rod inside the iridium ocean, and pipe it.
This will give you a reliable amount of iridium, and you can continue putting Submersibles anywhere you want.

Mid game

You will probably be in mid game after getting your production up, which is when you will want to defend yourself.
Mid game is typically the time people will attack you and your base, which is why you will want to have weapons,
turrets (not turrent), point defenses, and arc turrets.
There are a number of hand-held weapons you will need, an SMG, or an assault rifle, miniguns will do fine if you're defending.
Rocket launchers, shotguns, sniper rifles, a scanner, a laser drill, and upgrades will set you up for combat,
you will want to practice combat to make sure you don't mess up while defending you and your base.
Automatic defense is a must for any base, you will need a lot of turrets, as they will fend off nearby attackers,
Point defenses as an add-on to your wall, and arc turrets, which are short range, but can deal a lot of damage to enemies.
When attacking, you can make an artillery barge, they may look something like this:

These may be used to deal destructive damage to the enemy, as an artillery is extremely powerful.
You can also choose to use a laser drill to cut the supports of an enemy island, their bridge holding them up, and the spatial anchor.

Late game

Late game will usually only happen for a brief while, but if teams are finally meeting their challanges, it could take a while.
Laser strikes may be easily constructed as iridium production is increased,
and a lucky team may have occupied the beacon block, which when constructed, takes 150 seconds to initialize a shower of laser strikes.
This is when the game can get extremely laggy, and when skilled players will take advantage of the slow fire rate of several defenses.
Adding as many arc turrets and turrets as possible will easily counteract the slow fire rate of defenses.
